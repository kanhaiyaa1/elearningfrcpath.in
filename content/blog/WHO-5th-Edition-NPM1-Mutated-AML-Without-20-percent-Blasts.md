## The Game-Changer You Must Know

The **WHO 5th Edition (WHO-HEM5)** has eliminated the 20% blast requirement for diagnosing AML with NPM1 mutation. This is a high-yield, direct exam target for both **FRCPath Part 1** and **NEET-SS Pathology**.

> **Exam Alert:** Any question presenting a patient with <20% blasts + NPM1 mutation + myeloid morphology = AML by WHO-HEM5 criteria. Do not let the blast count mislead you.

---

## Why Did WHO Remove the Threshold?

The 20% blast threshold was a pragmatic historical cutoff — not biologically meaningful for NPM1-mutated disease. Clinical studies showed that patients with NPM1-mutated myeloid neoplasms with **<20% blasts** have:

- **Shorter overall survival** compared to patients with wild-type NPM1 and similar blast counts
- **Better outcomes** with upfront AML-type intensive chemotherapy (compared to MDS-directed regimens)
- Identical **gene expression profiles** to NPM1-mutated AML with ≥20% blasts

Waiting for 20% blasts meant delayed diagnosis, inappropriate treatment, and worse prognosis — the threshold was doing more harm than good.

## Key Morphologic Clues for NPM1-Mutated AML

Look for these features on bone marrow biopsy and aspirate:

- **Myelomonocytic or monocytic differentiation** — the most common morphologic pattern
- **Cup-like nuclear invaginations** in >10% of blasts — highly specific for NPM1 mutation
- **CD34 negativity** in approximately 80% of cases — atypical for most AML subtypes
- **Multilineage dysplasia** may be present but does **not** change the classification or prognosis under WHO-HEM5

## IHC Shortcut — Clinically Useful

**Cytoplasmic NPM1 staining by immunohistochemistry (IHC)** can serve as a surrogate marker for NPM1 mutation.

- Normal NPM1 is **nuclear**
- Mutated NPM1 mislocalises to the **cytoplasm**
- Cytoplasmic staining on a trephine biopsy = high suspicion for NPM1 mutation
- Useful when molecular testing results are delayed or unavailable

---

## Classification Under WHO-HEM5

Under the **WHO Classification of Haematolymphoid Tumours, 5th Edition (2022)**:

- The entity is renamed: **"AML with NPM1 mutation"**
- **No blast threshold required** — even <20% blasts qualifies if NPM1 mutation is confirmed
- This entity is distinct from AML-MRC (myelodysplasia-related changes) — NPM1 mutation **takes precedence**
- NPM1-mutated AML is retained as a **defining genetic abnormality** regardless of blast count

## Exam Pearl — Practice This Question

> **Q:** A 55-year-old presents with fatigue and pancytopenia. Bone marrow aspirate shows **15% blasts** with cup-like nuclear invaginations. Flow cytometry: CD34−, CD33+, CD117+. IHC shows cytoplasmic NPM1 positivity.
>
> **What is the diagnosis?**

**A: AML with NPM1 mutation** — despite <20% blasts, WHO-HEM5 diagnostic criteria are fully met. The patient should receive AML-type induction chemotherapy, not MDS-directed therapy.
