Something shifted in breast pathology reporting rooms over the past two years that has not yet fully reached many exam preparation resources. The binary world of **HER2-positive versus HER2-negative** — which governed breast cancer reporting for two decades — has fractured. In its place, a four-tier spectrum is emerging: HER2-null, HER2-ultralow, HER2-low, and HER2-positive. And the distinction between a score of **IHC 0 and IHC 1+** — previously clinically irrelevant — is now one of the most consequential calls a breast pathologist makes.

For **FRCPath and NEET-SS 2026 candidates**, this is not background reading. The DESTINY-Breast04 and DESTINY-Breast06 trial results, the 2023 ASCO/CAP guideline update, and the 2025 International Expert Consensus on HER2 reporting have together made **HER2-low and HER2-ultralow breast cancer** a near-certain exam topic — in both the content and the IHC interpretation format. This article gives you the mechanistic depth, scoring precision, and exam-ready framework to answer any question in this space.

---

## Why This Topic Is Exam-Relevant Right Now

Three concurrent developments have pushed HER2-low onto exam papers in 2025–2026:

1. **DESTINY-Breast04 (NEJM, 2022):** Trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd, Enhertu) demonstrated a significant survival benefit in previously treated **HER2-low** metastatic breast cancer — median PFS 13.2 vs 8.1 months vs chemotherapy (HR 0.62; p<0.001). FDA approval followed. This was the first trial to prove that breast cancers with as little as IHC 1+ HER2 expression are therapeutically targetable.

2. **DESTINY-Breast06 (2024):** T-DXd extended its benefit to **HER2-ultralow** (IHC 0 with faint incomplete staining in ≤10% of cells) in HR-positive metastatic breast cancer, doubling progression-free survival vs chemotherapy. FDA and EMA extended T-DXd approval to the ultralow category in 2024–2025.

3. **International Expert Consensus on HER2 Reporting (Modern Pathology, 2025):** A global panel — including the UK National Coordinating Committee of Breast Pathology (NCCBP), Association of Breast Pathology (ABP), and International Society of Breast Pathology (ISBP) — published standardised definitions and scoring recommendations for HER2-low and ultralow categories.

**Exam translation:** Any question involving an IHC 0 breast cancer result, a "HER2-negative" patient being considered for T-DXd, or the distinction between null/ultralow/low is now fair game. If you cannot confidently score HER2 across the new spectrum, you will lose marks on some of the highest-discrimination questions in the paper.

## The Old Binary System — and Why It Broke

For most of the 2000s and 2010s, breast pathologists reported HER2 in three meaningful categories:

| Old category | IHC score | ISH | Clinical meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| HER2-positive | 3+ (or 2+/ISH+) | Amplified (for 2+) | Eligible for trastuzumab, pertuzumab, T-DM1 |
| HER2-negative | 1+ or 2+/ISH− | Not amplified | No HER2-targeted therapy |
| HER2-negative | 0 | Not amplified | No HER2-targeted therapy |

Both IHC 0 and IHC 1+ fell into the same clinical bucket: "HER2-negative, no targeted therapy." The distinction between 0 and 1+ had no therapeutic consequence, so reproducibility was not prioritised.

**The problem:** This meant pathologists inconsistently scored the boundary between 0 and 1+. Some labs scored faint incomplete staining in a minority of cells as 0; others scored the same slide as 1+. When that distinction became clinically critical — determining T-DXd eligibility — interobserver variability became a patient safety issue.

---

## The New Spectrum: Four Tiers You Must Know

The emerging framework replaces the old two-bucket negative category with four discrete tiers:

| New tier | IHC score | Precise definition | ISH | Clinical action (2025–2026) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HER2-null** | 0 | No staining whatsoever — complete absence of membrane reactivity | Not required | Not eligible for T-DXd (currently) |
| **HER2-ultralow** | 0 (with staining) | Faint/barely perceptible **incomplete** membrane staining in **>0% to ≤10%** of tumour cells | Not required | T-DXd eligible (DESTINY-Breast06; HR+ mBC) |
| **HER2-low** | 1+ or 2+/ISH− | Faint incomplete (1+) or weak-moderate complete (2+) staining; ISH non-amplified for 2+ | Non-amplified for 2+ | T-DXd eligible (DESTINY-Breast04 and 06) |
| **HER2-positive** | 3+ or 2+/ISH+ | Intense complete membrane staining in >10% of cells (3+), or 2+ with HER2 gene amplification | Amplified for 2+/ISH+ | Trastuzumab, pertuzumab, T-DM1, T-DXd |

**The critical distinction:** The difference between **HER2-null (IHC 0, no staining)** and **HER2-ultralow (IHC 0 with staining)** is not a scoring category — it is the same IHC score of 0, subdivided by whether any membrane staining is visible at all. Both report as "IHC 0" in the traditional scoring system, but they now carry different therapeutic implications.

---

## ASCO/CAP 2023 Guideline Update: What It Actually Says

The 2023 ASCO/CAP HER2 Testing Guideline Update is frequently misquoted in revision materials. Here is exactly what it says:

> *"While it is premature to create new IHC result categories of HER2 expression (e.g., HER2-low, HER2-ultralow), best practices to distinguish IHC 0 from IHC 1+ are now clinically relevant because of the trial entry criteria that supported T-DXd's regulatory approval."*

**What this means in practice:**
- **ASCO/CAP does NOT officially endorse "HER2-low" as a formal reporting category** (as of 2023)
- However, accurately distinguishing IHC 0 from IHC 1+ is now a quality standard
- Labs must now review borderline cases at **40× magnification** to detect faint focal expression
- Second-opinion review is recommended for cases near the 0/1+ threshold
- Positive and negative controls with a **range of HER2 expression levels** (not just strong positive) are now required for QA

**Exam trap:** Questions may ask whether "HER2-low" is an official ASCO/CAP category. The answer (as of 2023 guidelines) is **no** — but the distinction between 0 and 1+ is now mandated as best practice. Distinguish the two.

## Scoring the New Spectrum: Practical Rules

### IHC Scoring at the Boundary — Rule Set

The precision required at the 0/1+/2+/3+ boundary has never been higher. Here is the rule set, consolidated from ASCO/CAP 2023 and the 2025 International Consensus:

**IHC 0 (HER2-null):**
- No staining **at all**
- OR staining in ≤10% of tumour cells with **no** membrane reactivity

**IHC 0+ (HER2-ultralow — within the IHC 0 score):**
- Faint or barely perceptible **incomplete** membrane staining in **>0% but ≤10%** of invasive tumour cells
- The key word: **any visible staining**, however faint, in any proportion of cells up to 10%
- Reports as "IHC 0" — but add a comment: *"faint incomplete membrane staining in <10% of cells (HER2-ultralow pattern)"*

**IHC 1+:**
- Faint/barely perceptible **incomplete** membrane staining in **>10%** of tumour cells
- Staining is perceptible only at high magnification
- Reports as "IHC 1+ (HER2-low)"

**IHC 2+ (equivocal):**
- Weak to moderate **complete** membrane staining in **>10%** of tumour cells
- **OR** strong complete staining in ≤10% of cells
- Reflex ISH required; if non-amplified → HER2-low; if amplified → HER2-positive

**IHC 3+ (positive):**
- Intense (strong) **complete** membrane staining in **>10%** of invasive tumour cells
- No ISH required

### The Completeness Rule — Exam Pearl

| Staining pattern | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Incomplete membrane staining (basolateral or lateral only) | 0, 0+, or 1+ (never 3+) |
| Complete membrane staining (circumferential) + faint/weak | 1+ or 2+ depending on intensity and % |
| Complete membrane staining + intense/strong + >10% cells | 3+ |

**The membrane must be circumferential (complete) AND intense AND >10% for 3+.** Any deviation drops the score.

## Pathology Practice Challenges — What Exams Test

### Interobserver Variability at the 0/1+ Boundary

Studies have consistently shown high interobserver variability at the IHC 0 vs 1+ threshold — higher than at any other boundary in HER2 scoring. A 2024 study (*Histopathology*, Tozbikian et al.) showed that even among experienced breast pathologists, reproducibility for HER2-low status is moderate at best (κ ~0.4–0.6 at the 0/1+ boundary).

**Why this happens:**
- Faint, incomplete staining in <10% of cells is subjectively difficult to distinguish from background
- Tissue processing, fixation time, and antibody clone affect signal intensity at low expression levels
- Pre-existing habit of "rounding down" borderline cases to 0 (when 0 and 1+ had the same clinical meaning)

**Exam angle:** Questions may ask about QA measures to improve 0/1+ reproducibility. Know these: review at 40×, second review for borderline cases, use of expression-range controls, central lab confirmation for trial entry.

### Specimen Type Effects

HER2-low status can differ between primary tumour and metastatic biopsy, and between core biopsy and excision specimen. Studies show **HER2-low to non-low conversion** (and vice versa) in approximately 20–30% of paired primary/metastatic specimens. This spatial and temporal heterogeneity means:

- A patient declared HER2-null on a primary excision may be HER2-low on a metastatic biopsy
- Re-testing at the time of metastatic disease is clinically relevant
- The 2025 Expert Consensus recommends **restesting HER2 on metastatic tissue** when the primary was scored as borderline 0/1+

## HER2-Low in the Context of Molecular Subtypes

HER2-low is not evenly distributed across breast cancer molecular subtypes. This matters for exam vignettes:

| Molecular subtype | Approximate HER2-low prevalence |
|---|---|
| HR+/HER2− (Luminal A/B) | ~55–65% are HER2-low |
| TNBC (triple-negative) | ~35–40% are HER2-low |
| HER2+ (amplified) | By definition, not applicable |

**Key fact:** The majority of HR+/HER2− breast cancers — previously the largest "untargetable" subgroup — are now either HER2-low or HER2-ultralow, making them eligible for T-DXd. This has fundamentally changed the treatment landscape for metastatic HR+ breast cancer.

**TNBC angle:** Approximately 35–40% of triple-negative breast cancers are HER2-low. For a disease with limited targeted options, T-DXd in HER2-low TNBC (also shown in DESTINY-Breast04) represents a meaningful new treatment option — and the pathological classification that enables or denies access to it.

## Exam Pearls & Mnemonics

**Mnemonic — "FULL CIRCLE means 3+" (for IHC scoring speed):**
> A score of **3+** requires ALL THREE: **F**ull circumferential (complete) staining + **U**niform intensity (strong) + **L**arge proportion (>10% of cells). If any one of the three is absent, the score cannot be 3+.

**Mnemonic — HER2 spectrum from bottom to top: "Nobody Understands Low Positivity"**
> **N**ull (IHC 0, no staining) → **U**ltralow (IHC 0+, faint incomplete ≤10%) → **L**ow (IHC 1+, or 2+/ISH−) → **P**ositive (IHC 3+, or 2+/ISH+)

**Pearl — The "comment rule" for ultralow:**
IHC 0 cases with any faint incomplete staining must carry a **comment** noting the ultralow pattern — the score alone (0) no longer conveys the information needed for treatment decisions. Failure to comment on faint staining in a 0-scored case is now a QA deficiency.

**Pearl — ASCO/CAP 2023 does NOT use the term "HER2-low" as a formal category:**
This is a deliberate exam trap. The correct answer to "Which statement about HER2-low is TRUE?" may include: "ASCO/CAP 2023 guidelines do not formally define HER2-low as a reporting category, but mandate best practices to distinguish IHC 0 from 1+."

**Pearl — T-DXd is an antibody-drug conjugate (ADC), not a naked antibody:**
T-DXd = trastuzumab (anti-HER2 antibody) + deruxtecan (topoisomerase I inhibitor payload). It is distinct from trastuzumab (Herceptin), pertuzumab, and T-DM1 (ado-trastuzumab emtansine). The high drug-to-antibody ratio (DAR ~8) and bystander killing effect mean it works at lower HER2 expression levels than T-DM1 (DAR ~3.5) — this is the mechanism that explains why T-DXd works in HER2-low/ultralow where T-DM1 does not.

## Practice MCQs

**Q1.** A 54-year-old female undergoes core biopsy of a 2 cm breast mass. IHC for HER2 shows faint, barely perceptible, incomplete membrane staining in approximately 8% of invasive tumour cells. Staining is absent in the remaining tumour cells. ISH is not performed. According to current (2025) expert consensus, the correct report should state:

- A) HER2 IHC score 0 — HER2-negative
- B) HER2 IHC score 1+ — HER2-low, eligible for trastuzumab
- C) HER2 IHC score 0, with a comment noting faint incomplete membrane staining in <10% of cells (HER2-ultralow pattern)
- D) HER2 IHC score 2+ (equivocal) — reflex ISH required

<details>
<summary>View Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Answer: C — HER2 IHC score 0, with a comment noting faint incomplete membrane staining in <10% of cells (HER2-ultralow pattern)**

This case describes the **HER2-ultralow** pattern: faint, barely perceptible, incomplete membrane staining in >0% but ≤10% of tumour cells. Under current ASCO/CAP scoring, this still reports as IHC score **0** — there is no official "0+" category in the formal scoring system. However, the 2025 International Expert Consensus mandates a **comment** documenting the presence of any faint incomplete staining, as this distinguishes HER2-null (score 0, no staining) from HER2-ultralow (score 0 with staining), which now has different treatment implications (DESTINY-Breast06). Option B is incorrect: IHC 1+ requires faint incomplete staining in **>10%** of cells. Option D is incorrect: 2+ equivocal requires weak-moderate complete membrane staining in >10% of cells, not faint incomplete staining. Trastuzumab (option B label) is for HER2-positive, not HER2-low — T-DXd is the relevant agent for HER2-low/ultralow.

</details>

**Q2.** A breast oncologist asks you to re-review a metastatic liver biopsy from a patient whose primary breast cancer (HR+) was reported as "HER2 IHC 0" two years ago. The patient progressed through endocrine therapy and is being considered for trastuzumab deruxtecan. The metastatic biopsy IHC shows faint incomplete membrane staining in 15% of invasive tumour cells. Which statement is CORRECT?

- A) The patient is not eligible for T-DXd because her primary tumour was HER2-null
- B) HER2 status should always be reported from the primary tumour; metastatic re-testing is not recommended
- C) The metastatic biopsy scores IHC 1+ (HER2-low); this finding supersedes the primary result and is relevant to T-DXd eligibility
- D) HER2 IHC 1+ on a metastatic biopsy is insufficient to confirm HER2-low status — ISH amplification testing is required for all 1+ cases

<details>
<summary>View Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Answer: C — The metastatic biopsy scores IHC 1+ (HER2-low); this finding supersedes the primary result and is relevant to T-DXd eligibility**

Faint incomplete membrane staining in >10% of cells = IHC **1+** (HER2-low). The 2025 Expert Consensus and current clinical guidelines recommend **retesting HER2 on metastatic tissue** when the primary was borderline or when HER2-low/ultralow status would affect treatment eligibility. HER2 status can change between primary and metastatic sites — studies show ~20–30% discordance — so primary tumour HER2 scoring does not definitively rule out HER2-low at a metastatic site. Option A incorrectly treats the primary result as definitive. Option B is the old practice standard — retesting metastatic tissue is now specifically recommended. Option D is incorrect: ISH (reflex testing) is required for IHC **2+** (equivocal), not for IHC 1+. An IHC 1+ result is definitively HER2-low without ISH.

</details>

**Q3.** Which of the following mechanisms best explains why trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) is clinically effective in HER2-low breast cancer when trastuzumab and T-DM1 are not?

- A) T-DXd has a higher affinity for the HER2 extracellular domain than trastuzumab, allowing binding at low receptor density
- B) T-DXd has a high drug-to-antibody ratio (~8) and its topoisomerase I inhibitor payload has a bystander killing effect on adjacent HER2-negative tumour cells
- C) T-DXd activates complement-dependent cytotoxicity more efficiently than trastuzumab at low HER2 expression levels
- D) T-DXd bypasses HER2 receptor binding entirely and acts directly on topoisomerase I within tumour cells

<details>
<summary>View Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Answer: B — T-DXd has a high drug-to-antibody ratio (~8) and its topoisomerase I inhibitor payload has a bystander killing effect on adjacent HER2-negative tumour cells**

This is the mechanistic explanation for T-DXd's efficacy in HER2-low/ultralow tumours — and it is a high-yield FRCPath and NEET-SS concept. **Trastuzumab (DAR ~0)** works via ADCC and HER2 signalling blockade — requires dense HER2 expression for meaningful effect. **T-DM1 (DAR ~3.5, maytansinoid DM1 payload)** is also insufficient at low HER2 density; the drug-to-antibody ratio is too low to deliver enough cytotoxic payload when HER2 receptors are sparse. **T-DXd (DAR ~8, deruxtecan/exatecan payload)**: (1) The high DAR means each antibody-HER2 binding event delivers 8 cytotoxic payload molecules; (2) Deruxtecan is a membrane-permeable topoisomerase I inhibitor — once released inside the HER2-low cell, it diffuses into adjacent cells (**bystander effect**), killing HER2-null cells in the vicinity. This bystander mechanism amplifies T-DXd's effect beyond the HER2-expressing cells themselves, making low receptor density less limiting. Option A is incorrect — binding affinity is not the primary explanation. Option C is incorrect — CDC is a minor mechanism for trastuzumab and not the relevant distinction. Option D is incorrect — T-DXd still requires HER2 receptor binding for internalisation.

</details>

## FAQs

**Q: Is "HER2-low" now an official pathology reporting category under ASCO/CAP guidelines?**

No — as of the 2023 ASCO/CAP HER2 Testing Guideline Update, "HER2-low" is not an officially endorsed reporting category. The guidelines state it would be premature to introduce new IHC result categories. However, the same guidelines mandate that distinguishing IHC 0 from IHC 1+ is now clinically relevant and must follow best practice scoring criteria. In practice, many labs and clinical teams use the term "HER2-low" informally to denote IHC 1+ or 2+/ISH− results, and the 2025 International Expert Consensus has moved further toward formalising the terminology. For exam purposes: ASCO/CAP 2023 = no formal category; clinical practice and expert consensus = "HER2-low" widely used and therapeutically actionable.

---

**Q: If a breast cancer is scored IHC 2+ on HER2, when is it "HER2-low" and when is it "HER2-positive"?**

ISH (in situ hybridisation — FISH or CISH) is the determinant. An IHC 2+ result is **equivocal** and always requires reflex ISH testing: if ISH shows **HER2 gene non-amplification** → HER2-low (IHC 2+/ISH−); if ISH shows **HER2 gene amplification** → HER2-positive (IHC 2+/ISH+). The IHC score alone is insufficient for IHC 2+ cases — never report a final HER2 status as "positive" or "low" based on IHC 2+ without ISH results.

---

**Q: Can a tumour be HER2-ultralow and triple-negative at the same time?**

Yes. Triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) is defined by ER−, PR−, and HER2-negative status by traditional scoring. Approximately 35–40% of TNBCs are HER2-low (IHC 1+ or 2+/ISH−), and a proportion are HER2-ultralow (IHC 0 with faint staining). These tumours are still technically "HER2-negative" by conventional reporting — they are triple-negative — but they are no longer "HER2-null." The DESTINY-Breast04 trial included HR−/HER2-low patients (predominantly TNBC), demonstrating T-DXd benefit. So a TNBC patient could be correctly described as triple-negative (conventional reporting) while also being HER2-low and T-DXd eligible — a paradox the exam may test.

---

**Q: What is the bystander effect of T-DXd and why is it exam-relevant?**

The bystander effect refers to the ability of T-DXd's payload (deruxtecan — a topoisomerase I inhibitor) to diffuse across cell membranes after being released inside a HER2-expressing cell and kill adjacent HER2-negative tumour cells. This is mechanistically important because: (1) it explains efficacy in HER2-low tumours where receptor density is insufficient for direct cytotoxic delivery alone; (2) it addresses tumour heterogeneity — HER2-negative cells within a HER2-low tumour are killed by payload diffusion; (3) it distinguishes T-DXd from T-DM1, whose maytansinoid payload is not membrane-permeable and has no bystander effect. Exam questions have asked about this distinction directly — "which property of T-DXd explains its efficacy in HER2-low tumours where T-DM1 is ineffective?"

---

**Q: How does HER2-low status affect the pathology report structure in routine breast cancer practice?**

Currently, most routine pathology reports still use the traditional IHC 0/1+/2+/3+ scoring system. The practical additions recommended by the 2025 Expert Consensus are: (1) for IHC 0 cases, add a comment stating whether any faint staining is present or entirely absent (to distinguish null from ultralow); (2) for equivocal IHC 2+ cases, always include reflex ISH results; (3) avoid "rounding down" borderline 0/1+ cases without documentation. Formal introduction of "HER2-low" and "HER2-ultralow" as distinct reporting categories in standard pathology reports is anticipated but has not yet been universally mandated by ASCO/CAP as of 2025. ## Key Takeaways

- **HER2 is no longer binary** — the spectrum from null to ultralow to low to positive has replaced the old negative/positive dichotomy, and the distinction between each tier now has direct treatment implications
- **HER2-null (IHC 0, no staining)** vs **HER2-ultralow (IHC 0 with faint incomplete staining ≤10%)**: same score, different clinical meaning — add a comment to distinguish them
- **HER2-low = IHC 1+, or IHC 2+/ISH−**: both tiers are T-DXd eligible (DESTINY-Breast04)
- **HER2-ultralow** is now also T-DXd eligible in HR+ metastatic breast cancer (DESTINY-Breast06)
- **ASCO/CAP 2023**: does not formally endorse "HER2-low" as a category but mandates 0 vs 1+ distinction as best practice — know this nuance cold
- **T-DXd mechanism**: high DAR (~8) + bystander effect (membrane-permeable payload) = efficacy at low HER2 density; this distinguishes it from trastuzumab and T-DM1
- **Re-testing at metastasis**: HER2 status changes in 20–30% of primary → metastatic pairs; re-biopsy is recommended when HER2-low eligibility for T-DXd is in question
